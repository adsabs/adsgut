    The medical term for high blood cholesterol is lipid disorder, or hyperlipidemia. PubMed Wikipedia

    • Hyperlipidemia is diagnosed by a blood test.
    • Lipitor is prescribed in 69% of cases.
    • 34% of patients are diagnosed with heart disease within 5 years.

    How is hyperlipidemia diagnosed?
    A cholesterol test is done to diagnose a lipid disorder. Some national guidelines recommend having your first screening cholesterol test at age 20. Everyone should have their first screening test by age 35 in men, and age 45 in women. PubMed Wikipedia
    How is hyperlipidemia prevented?
    Excerpt from PubMed:

    • Eat foods low in fat such as whole grains, fruits, and vegetables.
    • Avoid foods high in saturated fat.
    • Exercise regularly.
    • Lose weight if overweight.
    • Quit smoking.
